# Hantu Air

> Hantu Air ('water ghost') is the Malay/Indonesian name for an unseen inhabitant of rivers, lakes, swamps, and the sea in the animist traditions of Maritime Southeast Asia. It can be benevolent or malevolent depending on how humans treat the body of water it occupies.

## Overview

Hantu Air (literally 'water ghost' in Malay), also called Puaka Air or Mambang Air, is a category of supernatural entity in the animist substrate of Malay, Indonesian, Bruneian, and broader Maritime Southeast Asian belief. Hantu Air inhabits all kinds of water — rivers, lakes, swamps, ditches, the sea — and is sometimes said to be the lingering spirit of a person who drowned, though more commonly it is treated as an independent, place-bound entity. It is most often invisible but is reported to manifest as a floating log, a beautiful woman, a fish, or other animals to lure people into the water, where they drown or are consumed. The belief encodes practical knowledge about water hazards (currents, hidden snags, deep pools) and motivates respectful behavior toward bodies of water — including ritual offerings such as the East Coast Malaysian Semah Pantai sea ceremony, the last recorded instance of which occurred on 22 April 1960. In coastal and riverine communities, Hantu Air is treated as both a danger and a tutelary presence; correct ritual conduct is held to secure good fishing and safe passage, while disrespect or pollution invites illness, drowning, or other misfortune. The belief survives most strongly in rural Malay and Indonesian communities and has been syncretised with Islamic frameworks, in which the Hantu Air is often understood as a kind of jinn.

## Etymology

- **Origin**: Malay: hantu ('ghost' or 'spirit') + air ('water'). 'Puaka' and 'mambang' are alternative Malay terms for nature-spirits or genii loci.
- **Meaning**: Literally 'water ghost' or 'water spirit'.
- **First use**: Pre-Islamic Malay animism; documented in colonial-era ethnographies of Malay folklore (e.g. Skeat, 1900).

## Theories & Explanations

### Paranormal / Cryptozoological

**Animist genius loci** _(Traditional Malay folk belief, Bomoh and pawang practitioners)_

Within Malay animism, Hantu Air is a real spiritual inhabitant of bodies of water with whom humans must maintain ritual etiquette.

**Jinn interpretation** _(Islamic-Malay folk practitioners)_

Within Islamic-Malay syncretism, Hantu Air is reinterpreted as a class of jinn dwelling in water.
