## Research Note

This bibliography documents scholarly and cultural sources on Hantu Air (Malaysian water spirits). Sources span from colonial ethnographic records to contemporary environmental psychology, from Islamic theological texts to indigenous oral traditions.

**Important Considerations**:

1. **Colonial Bias**: Early documentation reflects colonial perspectives that often dismissed indigenous beliefs as "primitive superstitions"
2. **Religious Context**: Islamic framework shapes modern interpretation; understanding jinn theology essential
3. **Living Tradition**: These represent ongoing belief systems for many communities, not merely historical curiosities
4. **Language**: Many primary sources in Malay; translations may lose cultural nuances
5. **Oral Tradition**: Written sources capture only fraction of rich oral transmission
6. **Safety Wisdom**: Folklore encodes practical water safety knowledge in supernatural framework
7. **Environmental Knowledge**: Spirit geography maps objectively dangerous water features

**Acknowledgment**: This documentation draws on work of numerous scholars, indigenous knowledge keepers, bomoh practitioners, and community members who have preserved Malay water spirit traditions. We acknowledge the cultural custodianship of Malay communities, Orang Asli peoples, Iban, Kadazan-Dusun, and other indigenous groups whose living traditions these sources document.

**Special Thanks**: To the late Walter Skeat, Kirk Endicott, Carol Laderman, and Mohd. Taib Osman whose foundational scholarship made systematic study possible while maintaining cultural respect.
